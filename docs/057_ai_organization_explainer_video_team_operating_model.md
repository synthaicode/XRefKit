# AI Organization Explainer Video Team Operating Model

This page defines the operating model for the AI Organization Explainer Video Team.

This team is a Marketing Group production function. Its purpose is to explain the
XRefKit AI organization model in a short, understandable, slide-based video.

For day-to-day request format and production guidance, see
[AI Organization Explainer Video Team usage guide](051_parapara_video_creation_team_usage_guide.md#xid-5A7C31D9E842).
For the boundary among operating models, usage guides, and design pages, see
[Operating models, usage guides, and design pages](022_operating_models_guides_and_designs.md#xid-9C4E2A71D583).

## Purpose

The team converts the repository's AI organization concept into marketing-facing
explanation content.

The team does not exist merely to make videos. It exists to make the following
ideas understandable:

- XRefKit is an operating base for AI work, not a model-weight repository.
- AI work becomes more stable when responsibilities, knowledge, workflow, and
  checks are separated.
- AI organization is a way to reduce responsibility mixing, forgotten context,
  unsupported judgment, and untracked handoff.
- OR Team provides a continuous-improvement loop after normal execution teams
  have been organized.

## Ownership

This team belongs to Marketing Group work.

It may use Marketing Group slide-production skills such as
`marketing_slide_png`, but it has a broader video-explanation responsibility:

- message framing
- audience modeling
- scenario design
- slide design
- narration
- audio generation
- video assembly
- communication validation
- license and attribution display

Official announcements, press releases, and policy commitments remain outside
this team unless explicitly assigned by the responsible owner.

## Target Audiences

The default audiences are:

1. people evaluating whether to introduce AI into operational work
2. business or IT managers who need to understand why AI work needs structure
3. practitioners who already use AI individually but need a shared operating
   model
4. people who must explain this repository to another AI or stakeholder

When a request targets only one audience, the team must tune the video for that
audience instead of trying to serve every audience equally.

## Message Backbone

Unless the request explicitly defines another message, use this backbone:

1. normal AI use often starts as individual practice
2. individual practice and prompt sharing do not create stable operation by
   themselves
3. AI is useful but tends to forget, mix responsibilities, and drift in long
   workflows
4. real work is a bundle of tasks, checks, handoffs, and decisions
5. therefore multiple AI roles should be composed around responsibility
   boundaries
6. the repository manages responsibilities, knowledge, flows, skills, outputs,
   and evidence
7. OR Team closes the continuous-improvement loop by observing, improving, and
   re-observing the organization

## Internal Roles

### Marketing Lead AI

Owns the communication objective, target audience, desired viewer action, and
completion criteria.

Typical outputs:

- video objective
- target-audience definition
- viewer-action definition
- completion criteria

### Learner Model AI

Models what the audience is likely to misunderstand and controls explanation
level.

Typical outputs:

- explanation-level definition
- difficult-term list
- prohibited or supplemental expression list

### Repository Reading AI

Reads repository structure, documentation, responsibility boundaries, and
examples so the message is grounded in the repository.

Typical outputs:

- repository explanation memo
- terminology glossary
- claim-source map
- issue list

### Scenario AI

Builds the story order and assigns one clear message to each slide.

Typical outputs:

- chapter outline
- one-message-per-slide script
- slide order

### Slide Design AI

Defines what appears on each screen and decides how the message should be
represented visually.

Typical outputs:

- slide specification
- slide asset list
- text-to-visual mapping

### Narration AI

Writes spoken narration and estimates timing for each slide.

Typical outputs:

- narration script
- duration estimate
- reading-adjustment notes

### Diagram And Direction AI

Designs diagram structure, icon usage, visual density, and screen rhythm.

Typical outputs:

- visual direction notes
- diagram instructions
- density and pacing notes

### Consistency QA AI

Checks whether the message, repository facts, slide order, terminology, and
narration remain consistent.

Typical outputs:

- consistency findings
- correction-required judgment
- unresolved claim list

### Beginner QA AI

Checks whether a first-time viewer can understand the explanation without
additional verbal supplementation.

Typical outputs:

- beginner-perspective findings
- simplification suggestions
- confusing-term list

### Final Editing AI

Integrates corrected deliverables into the final distribution package.

Typical outputs:

- distribution slide deck
- generated audio assets
- edit timeline
- synchronized draft video
- final public video
- license and attribution slide

## Operating Loop

1. Confirm the audience, objective, target duration, publication context, and
   desired viewer action.
2. Read the repository materials needed to support the claims.
3. Define the message backbone and chapter sequence.
4. Produce one-message-per-slide scenario rows.
5. Produce slide specifications and visual assets.
6. Produce narration, timing estimates, and audio.
7. Assemble a synchronized draft video.
8. Run consistency QA and beginner QA.
9. Correct the scenario, slides, narration, or timing.
10. Add license and attribution display when required.
11. Finalize the distribution package.

## Completion Conditions

The team may close work only when:

- the video has a defined audience and viewer action
- every slide has one clear message
- every non-obvious claim is traceable to repository material or marked as a
  marketing explanation
- narration and slide message are aligned
- scene transitions do not cut speech unnaturally
- the total duration matches the requested range
- the final output explains XRefKit's AI organization model without implying
  unsupported product or policy claims
- required license and attribution display is present
- human sign-off is preserved when final acceptance is required

## Must Not Decide

- business-priority decisions outside the requested video objective
- approval of facts, claims, or policy positions that require an external owner
- official announcement wording unless assigned by the announcement owner
- final acceptance of the video when human sign-off is required
